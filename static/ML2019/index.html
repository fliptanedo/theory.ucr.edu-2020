FUNDAMENTAL PHYSICS INNOVATION AWARD

This page collects resources from a 2018 APS/Gorden & Betty Moore Foundation Fundamental Physics Innovation Lectureship Award by Flip Tanedo, Daniel Whiteson, and Ben Nachman to bring Ben to UC Riverside and UC Irvine to promote new techniques in machine learning in the search for new fundamental physics.

MACHINE LEARNING in HEP & BEYOND

Lectures by Ben Nachman, 13 - 19 January 2019

Machine learning (ML) has revolutionized many areas of industry. These techniques are also promising for advancing fundamental science. Correponding developments in high-energy physics (HEP) show that ML can be used to analyze high-dimensional data, accelerate simulations, and sidestep modeling limitations. These advances complement hardware innovations and reduce costs by increasing science reach for a fixed experimental size and run time.

The goal of these lectures is to communicate cutting edge techniques in ML to a mixed particle physics audience in order to identify new “killer applications” of ML in dark matter astrophysics, and new physics searches at smaller-scale experiments, and theoretical studies of high-dimensional parameter spaces. The audience included particle and astroparticle theory group at UC Riverside (PI Tanedo), the experimental high-energy physics group at UC Irvine (Co-PI Whiteson), and the data science neworks at each campus. The lectureship will build collaborative research between the PIs and help promote the research direction to the wider campus communities. The longer term goal is to encourage institutional support timely a cross-campus and interdisciplinary collaborations at the interface of particle physics and data science.

Dr. Ben Nachman


Dr. Benjamin Nachman is a post-doc in high-energy physics at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. He is part of the Large Hadron Collider's ATLAS Collaboration, where he studies radiation damage to silicon sensors (RD50) and the optimal readout of signals from those sensors (RD53).

He works at the interface of fundamental physics and applied statistics by applying advanced machine learning techniques to the physics of the strong force. He was recently a fellow at the Simons Institute for the Theory of Computing in the real time decision making program.
