SoCal BSM 2017

Sunday, April 2, 2017; 9:00 am - 5:30 pm; UCR Physics Building

Connecting the Southern California "Beyond the Standard Model" Community

SoCal BSM 2017 is the first of an annual set of one-day meetings for the Southern California particle theory community to re-connect, collaborate, and coordinate. This meeting is hosted by UC Riverside Particle Theory. Due to limited space, this event is invitation only.

Event Information

The workshop is intended to be accessible as a day trip. For those who would like to arrive early or stay an extra night, we have UC discount rates at the historic Mission Inn and very affordable 2-bed dormitory-style accomodations at the UCR extension. For more information, contact .

Parking is in Lot 13 (see map below).
If you have a UC parking permit of any type, you may park direclty in Lot 13. If you do not have a UC parking permit, we will have a staff member (Jhon Gonzalez) at the Lot 10 dispenser from 8:45am - 9:15am; see the map below.

Note that immediately after SoCalBSM, UC Irvine will be hosting the international "DM at LHC" conference. Irvine is one hour south of Riverside and you are encouraged to consider participating in that conference following SoCalBSM.

First Meeting Goals

We loosely base our meeting on the Southern California Strings Seminar and PIKIO. We will have talks from new faculty and postdocs/grad students.

  • Re-connect with colleagues
  • Highlight exciting research and junior physicists
  • Initiate collaborative research
  • Coordinate inter-campus initiatives
